NON-NEGOTIABLES
- The Hebrew/Greek source text is the final authority for meaning, syntax, number, gender, emphasis, and grammatical force.
- Use a formal-equivalence approach: preserve grammatical force; avoid paraphrase; do not add interpretive expansions.
- Use Modern Sacral English: modern grammar with dignified, elevated cadence suitable for public reading and memorization.

  - Do not use casual, slangy, or therapeutic language.
- Beauty is a constraint, not decoration:
  - Preserve parallelism, repetition, and rhythm where present in the original.
  - In historically memorized or liturgically familiar passages, prefer recognizable phrasing where it is faithful and excellent English.
  - Never sacrifice literal meaning for style.

PATRIARCHAL PRESERVATION CLAUSE
- Preserve masculine, representative, and covenantal language rather than neutralizing it.
